My Role

Product Designer

My Team

Team of 5 student for Human Centered Design Methods class

Timeline

August 2022 - December 2022

Facilitate emotive and contextual sharing of moments between parents and children once they move away to college.

The Challenge

Identifying the Product Opportunity Gaps and defining the challenge.

The team started with the broad concept of memory sharing. We identified ~50 POGs (Product Opportunity Gaps). These POGs aimed to capture untapped market potential by targeting unresolved user pain points. The ideas came from a variety of sources: listening to conference talks, the reading of research papers and articles, practicing augmentative writing, and reflecting on personal experiences and stories. We used affinity mapping and weighted matrix to narrow down the POG to the one mentioned above.

Research

We went out and talked to people.

Synthesis

How would the user journey for this problem look?

From the user research, we were able to identify the user journey map.

We identified our personas

From the insights from the user research, we considered four personas for the project: the independent graduate, the far-away one, the empty nester, and the working parent. We identified two more personas: the helicopter parent and the first mover. However, we have decided to exclude these from the scope as they have radically different needs, such as constant contact.

Product Requirements: We converted our research findings into aspects our solution must, could, and should have.

The majority of our must-haves fell under emotion and identity. Emotion must-haves centered around the ability to share emotions and feel emotionally at peace, knowing relatives are safe and well. Identity must-haves centered around the idea of 360 contexts to allow users to share when and where events are happening and express that through their own personal lens

Ideation

We brainstormed 50+ ideas

Each member created approximately 10 concepts using Analogical Reasoning, Forced Analogy, Biomimicry, and other processes from class. This led to a total of 51 ideas.

Narrowing down the ideas

We seperated the ideas into categories using Affinity mapping, this helped us to combine similar ideas, remove seemingly peripheral to the POG and creation of a "feature graveyard" - a list of features unique to eliminated ideas which remained interesting and applicable to the final selection. We followed this up with dot voting. Each team member was allocated five votes to assign to the ideas they evaluated and expected to best meet personal/team goals and the POG. We narrowed it down to 14 ideas.

VOA & Technology Target

Each remaining idea was scored based on the quantity of VOA must-haves it seemed to satisfy. The ideas were arranged on a target with highest scoring concepts at the center (to represent highest impact). The target was divided into three levels of digital intensity: fully digital, hybrid, and fully physical. Finally, the six most central concepts, and predicted to have the highest impact, were selected for further analysis.

Pugh Chart

A pugh chart was used as a quantitative analysis selection tool with the goal of mitigating bias and ensuring all relevant criteria were considered. All ideas were compared against the baseline design, Interactive Wall Apparatus.

Introducing our final concept

A minimalist device that displays critical messages to loved ones, via visual and tactile alerts. The unique feature of the device is that it can be stuck to any surface like a book, water bottle or key chain. Is is a way to ensure that parents and childeren can stay connected.

Rapid Prototype

We tried out a few rapid prototypes

Low fidelity prototypes developed in a 30 minute span to gain insight in product use types and to aid in producing questions for further user interviews. We tried out different form factors and docking positions for rumifico using rapid paper prototyping.

Rumifico

We present to you, RUMIFICO

How it serves it's purpose?

Functionality

  • Easily communicates availability and unavailability.
  • Messages are completely customizable allowing full self expression.
  • The companion app combined with Rumifico hardware streamlines communication as it allows individuals to filter out all extra notifications and noise a smartphone provides.

USABILITY

  • Nostalgia design allows for individuals who struggle with newer technology to engage seamlessly.
  • Companion app is streamlined to the basic necessities.

VISUAL DESIGN
