Team Project - 2020

Compass

Issue Module Redesign

Microsoft

Overview

Redesign the existing issue module in Compass to make it more user centric. The current design had a lot of gaps and usability issues, with this redesign we aim to address and rectify these gaps.

My Role

UX Researcher

My Team

1 Researcher, 1 Designer and lots of developers

Timeline

October 2020-December 2020

The Challenge

Compass is Microsoftâ€™s datacenter construction management solution. The issue module is one of the widely used features in Compass, but this is also the module that gets the most number of user tickets.

  1. Issue management page has too many details in it, some are capturing/displaying redundant or not users information as well.
  2. User is entering 22 out of 64 fields of data, ie 20.5% of the whole fields. Furthermore, there are lots of hidden fields and grids. No one knows what they do or why they are in the form.
  3. The flow becomes stuck at places such that the users are not able to proceed with their tasks.
  4. Multiple places had really big usability issues and gaps in the flows which forced the users to move outside the application to get things done.

We decided that it was time for a redesign of the module to increasing the user experience. I lead the user study, user research and insights part of the redesign.

User Research & Insights

An extensive study on our competitors showed that we were lagging in many aspects. We were playing catch up. There were lots of functionalities that were missing. I conducted user interviews with users across the different regions (EMEA, APAC and AMERS) and having different access permissions in the module, this made me realize that Compass has many gaps and pain points.

From studying and discussing the experience map with the team, I was able to identify the, major areas and gaps that needed focus.

  1. Too many clicks, too confusing.
  2. Redundant and duplicate data.
  3. No way to pin issues to the site map.
  4. Issue verification step has too many gaps.
  5. Difficult to keep track of issues.
  6. Too many email ping pongs outside the system.
  7. File upload was an experience nightmare.

Personas

I was able to identify 2 personas and create a storyboard for each of them, laying out a typical day at work. With the storyboard, I was able to present my findings and how they translate into real-life scenarios of the personas. This was a huge hit with the stakeholders and it helped in bringing them over to a â€˜YESâ€™ for the redesign.

Storyboards

Ralph's Storyboard

Ralph's Pain points

  • Ralph must keep his own personal notes to keep track of what all is happening in the issues.
  • Ralph has trouble finding out where each equipment for an issue is located.
  • Ralph keeps forgetting to add the after picture.
  • Ralph must periodically check his email to see if he has any communication on the issues from Carla.

Carla's Storyboard

Carla's Pain points

  • Carla has to keep referring to her notes to keep track of what all communications happened in the issue.
  • Carla has to check her notes if an issue needs retest.
  • Carla finds that Ralph keeps forgetting to add the after pic to the issues that he sends to her for approval.
  • Carla has to periodically go to the email app to send any communication and keep track of email communications.

Revamp

The research insights and storyboards were handed over to another designer in the team to lead the prototype and iteration. We had multiple design discussions, iteration, and scraping things till we finally came to a design that the users were able to navigate and make sense of without any difficulty.

Before

After

Removed out redundant and confusing data, to have only the relevant data in the UI. Adding before and after picture flow has been integrated as a part of the issue fix flow so that it will not be missed out by the user. Facilitated a clear and dedicated place for communicating among members.

Before

After

Completely redesigned the notes feature to make it easier to use for the user. Bringing in the common design elements that users are familiar with, like drag and drop and file preview will give a better experience for the users.

Going Forward

We have handed over the design to the engineering team. The team has started the development and targetting shipment in January or February of 2021.

My Learnings

  1. Working in collaboration with other designers: I learned that you don't need to do the whole design process to make a major impact.
  2. Convincing stakeholders: People tend to resonate better when you tell them a story rather than just hardcore numbers. Show how your new design would improve the matrix after telling the story has more impact.
  3. Not everyone would have the whole context: Not everyone who you are trying to convince will have the full context. It always helps to start from the very beginning, giving existing context and pain points.
  4. Working with multiple designers makes the design better: We had discussions on each element of the design and why it was designed that way.
  5. Users are always ready to tell you what is not working in your product: Users are more than willing to tell you where they are getting stuck in the product.
