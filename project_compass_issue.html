Team Project

Compass

Issue Management Redesign

Microsoft

Compass, Microsoft's Azure data center construction management solution, focuses on optimizing resource tracking and progress monitoring. One crucial backbone of this solution is the Issue Management module. This module plays a vital role in tracking and managing commissioning issues identified on-site or during testing. It streamlines the documentation, progress, prioritization and resolution of the issues identified. It is one of the most widely used module.


The redesign of the Compass issue management module, optimizing issue identification, tracking, and resolution helped achieve a monthly time savings of 50-80 man hours.

Team

Team consisting of Project Manager, Engineering Manager, Developers and Designer

Role

User Study, UX Research, Synthesis, Ideation, Prototyping

Time Frame

1.5 years

The Challenge

It was time for a redesign of the Issue module.

The issue module is one of the widely used features in Compass, but this is also the module that gets the most number of user tickets.

  1. The issue management page has too many details in it. Some are capturing/displaying redundant or not users information as well.
  2. The user is entering 22 out of 64 fields, ie 20.5% of the whole fields. Furthermore, there are lots of hidden fields and grids. No one knows what they do or why they are in the form.
  3. The flow becomes stuck at places such that the users are not able to proceed with their tasks.
  4. Multiple places had gaps in the flows which forced the users to move outside the application to get things done.

Research

A quick investigation revealed numerous issues and gaps within the current state of the module.

An extensive study of our competitors showed that we were lagging in many aspects. We were playing catch-up. There were lots of functionalities that were missing. We conducted user interviews with users across different regions (EMEA, APAC, and AMERS), and had different access permissions in the module. It made me realize that Compass has many gaps and pain points.

The major areas and gaps that needed focus.

From studying and discussing the experience map with the team, We were able to identify the, major areas and gaps that needed focus.

o1
Too many clicks, too confusing
o2
Redundant and duplicate data
o3
No way of keeping track of what was happening in an issue created
o4
Issue verification step has too many gaps and was confusing
o5
Difficult to keep track of issues
o6

o7

Some insights on what the users had to say during the research.

"An issue may not be looked at for a week. Each of us keep a note externally"

Ideation

During ideation, we identified two personas for our solution

Meet Ralph. He is an Electrical worker from General Electricals. He is currently working on the Microsoft data center construction project for the ABC site. He has a very carefree personality and always tends to forget things.

Meet Carla. She is the General Contractor for the ABC site of Microsoft data center construction. She is a fitness enthusiast and likes to ride her bike to work. She does not like when things keep her waiting.

The storyboard helped in bringing the stakeholders over to a 'YES' for the redesign.

We created a storyboard for each persona by laying out a typical day at work. With the storyboard, We presented my findings and how they translate into real-life scenarios of the personas. It was a huge hit with the stakeholders and it helped bring them over to a 'YES' for the redesign.

Ralph's Storyboard

  • Ralph must keep his own personal notes to keep track of what all is happening in the issues.
  • Ralph has trouble finding out where each equipment for an issue is located.
  • Ralph keeps forgetting to add the after picture.
  • Ralph must periodically check his email to see if he has any communication on the issues from Carla.

Carla's Storyboard

  • Carla has to keep referring to her notes to keep track of what all communications happened in the issue.
  • Carla has to check her notes if an issue needs retest.
  • Carla finds that Ralph keeps forgetting to add the after pic to the issues that he sends to her for approval.
  • Carla has to periodically go to the email app to send any communication and keep track of email communications.

Final Design

Creation of an issue

A key improvement we focused on was simplifying the issue creation process. Through a redesign, we streamlined the process by reducing the amount of information users needed to enter when creating an issue record. Our goal was to eliminate redundancy and minimize confusion for a lighter and more user-friendly experience.

Before

  • Chunky with little distinction between sections.
  • All the data is jumbled together which, made the creation of the issue record difficult.
  • The form has duplicate and unused fields.
  • Too many unrelated sections.

After

  • The new design has clear distiction between different sections, facilitating easier issue record creation.
  • All unused and redundent fields and sections are removed.

Audit History to keep track of activities

We created a more detailed and streamlined audit history view so that it's easier for the user to find what all happened in that record.

Before

  • Limited audits were available. The users can not see what all happened in the record.
  • The available audit contained limited details on the activity.
  • Searching was not available.
  • Users had to refresh the whole form to see the updated audit, this caused a lot of waiting time.

After

  • Capturing all the activities that happened in the record in the audit.
  • The audit contained more details about the activity.
  • Search and refresh functionality increased the usability of the feature.

Approval flow

The approval experience was redesigned completely. The approval process in issue is linear. Each approval is done sequentially. Any deviation from that will cause the process to get stuck.

Before

  • Primitive approval process. The approval sequence is not apparent to the users.
  • The process would get stuck in places due to incorrect approvals.

After

  • Only showing the users what they need to see at the moment.
  • Streamline the approval process so that the approvals are guard railed to happen one after the other.
  • Easy access to the complete history of the approvals.

Attachments

The notes upload experience was a mess. We crafted a new experience to make the attachment upload and attachment view easier to use.

Before

  • The old design was not intuitive about how to add an attachment and where to find archived attachments.
  • Attachments need to be added at different stages of the issue resolution process. The attachment part was separate from the rest of the issue functionalities. Thus made it hard to find.
  • It lacked functionalities like drag and drop for attachments and file preview for the uploaded attachments.

After

  • Allowing access to attachments from all points in the issue process in which attachments need to be added. Quick access to attachments from all the pages.
  • A clear indication of the actions that can be performed.
  • Drag and drop for new attachments and preview function for existing attachments.

Threaded Discussions

The introduction of threaded discussions in the Issue module has proven to be a valuable enhancement based on user feedback. Details about the functionality and user experience include:

Commenting and Discussions

Users can now add comments and engage in discussions directly within the issue record, eliminating the need for external communication channels and keeping all relevant information within the system.

Accessibility and Visibility:

The threaded discussions are accessible from all pages of the issue module, allowing users to easily find and reference discussions associated with specific issues. This enhances visibility and ensures that important decisions and updates are not missed.

Mentioning users:

Users can mention other individuals within the discussions to draw their attention or involve them in the conversation. This feature facilitates effective collaboration and ensures that relevant stakeholders are included in the discussion.

Reply to specific threads:

The threaded structure of discussions allows users to reply to specific threads, keeping related conversations together and making it easier to follow the flow of discussion. This helps maintain organization and clarity within the system.

Search functionality:

Users can search for specific people or discussions within the threaded discussions, making it convenient to find relevant information quickly. This feature enhances efficiency and saves time for users.

Regarding the UX experience, we designed the text box for the main discussion at the top, ensuring that the latest discussion appears first. This prioritizes the most recent activity and makes it easier for users to immediately see the latest updates. During usability testing, we discovered that users preferred pagination over scrolling for large discussions. This design choice gives users more control and allows for easier navigation within extensive discussions.

By addressing user pain points and incorporating user feedback through usability testing, we have created a threaded discussion feature that meets user expectations and provides a seamless user experience within the system's Issue module.

My Learnings

Some of the things that I learnt while working on this project.

  1. Working in collaboration with other designers: I learned that you don't need to do the whole design process to create an impact on the project.
  2. Convincing stakeholders: People tend to resonate better when you tell them a story rather than just hard-core numbers. Show how your new design would improve the matrix after telling the story has more impact.
  3. Not everyone would have the whole context: Not everyone who you are trying to convince will have the full context. It always helps to start from the very beginning, giving existing context and pain points.
  4. Working with multiple designers makes the design better: We had discussions on each element of the design and why it was designed that way.
  5. Users are always ready to tell you what is not working in your product: Users are more than willing to tell you where they are getting stuck in the product.
  6. Working with technical constraints: Creating and fighting for good UX when there is a technical constraint for the functionality development. Compromises are needed along the way, but there should not be any compromise in the experience that we provide to the user.
