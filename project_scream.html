"If earth could talk, it would scream" is an interactive art installation aimed at bringing awareness to the impact that every human step has on the earth. Impact on the earth is visualized as ripples that form under the feet of the person walking. The more they walk over a spot, the more violent the ripples become; this shows that our impact on the earth is cumulative. Stand still for a while and the ripples will start dying out. This represents that the earth heals but slowly. Our impact on the earth is created faster and more frequently than the earth is able to heal.

The audience is encouraged to interact with the piece barefoot since our inspiration started from there. We found that being barefoot makes a person more merciful (would think twice before squishing a bug) and more aware of each step that the person is taking. They are better connected to the earth and respect her more. Running barefoot is one of the basic things that we loved to do as kids and we have lost that connection with the earth as we grew up.

Scoping Out Project Direction

Starting from all different direction

We started with elaborating on the topics that we are interested in exploring. We want to explore the intersection of: Nature's response to human interaction, the physical manifestation of feelings, psychological influence (focus on urban spaces), fulfilling emotional void and the physical manifestations of health.

Selected Design

Visualizing the pressure that we are putting on nature by trampling all over it

A platform on which when the person walks on it produces a downward graph that represents the impact and magnitude of the pressure that the person is putting on the floor. The platform will be like a huge cuboid, where the person will be able to walk on top and the visualization of the pressure can be seen from the sides of the cuboid. The person walking will be covered by a screen so that any onlooker will only see the impact on the land and not the cause of the impact. We will be able to see the impact (from the previous interaction) even when no interaction is happening currently to show lingering impact. The graph will die down slowly if no interaction happens on it to show that Nature can heal itself if left alone. The graph created by the impact will be created fast but will die down slowly to show the unequal effect and restoration.

Alternate Option: Physical Tactile map, enclosure in which the sensors are on the floor but the impact graph is shown on the ceiling.

Materials: Build a platform that enables interaction, Mirrors, Pressure Sensors, Lots of wires.

Question: What is the specific impact that we are trying to show?

Critical Feedback: Visualizing pressure have the unintented consequence of broadcasting people's weight. Are you trying to convey that a person on the heavier side would have more impact on the earth?

Action: Instead of visualizing pressure, we changed to visualizing the impact we make on earth by walking over it for a long time.
