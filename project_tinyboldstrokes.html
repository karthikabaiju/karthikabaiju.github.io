Tiny Bold Strokes

As an artist, it's essential to have an e-commerce platform to showcase my artworks, and that's exactly what I did with Tiny Bold Strokes. We worked on developing the website and branding, with a clear vision in mind.


The main objective was to create a website that truly captured the essence of the brand, while ensuring that the artwork takes center stage.

Problem Space

How to create a website that highlights the artwork yet holds true to the brand?

Tiny Bold Strokes is all about the artwork. The logo, branding, social media layout are created according to minimalistic principles so that it would not distract the viewer from the artwork. We tried to create a website that reflected the brand's minimalistic principles.

Research

A study on the available online platforms and their depiction of the artist's brand.

Our comparative study examined how well online platforms enabled artists to represent their brand, emphasizing customer engagement and catering to artists' needs.

Etsy: Etsy is a popular platform for artists, but the high competition can make it challenging for shops to stand out. Limited customization options can hinder brand representation. To enhance visibility and establish a distinct brand identity, artists may need to explore additional strategies such as marketing efforts and creating independent websites. These steps can help artists gain more exposure and differentiate themselves in the crowded marketplace.


Saatchi Art: One platform exclusively for artists to sell art to collectors has a dedicated customer base, ensuring customer engagement. However, it lacks functionality to track customer engagement. Additionally, the platform offers no customization options, limiting brand identity representation for artists.


Shopify: The platform offers you the option to create your own store. It helps to display the brand but, it was too expensive for starters.

My Learnings

Some of the things that I learnt while working on this project.

  1. Visual design and UI design principles: This project provided me with the opportunity to learn about visual design, color palette, item placement, and typography.
  2. User expectations: When designing an experience like check out, it's always best to stick to the flow that the users are familiar with since they will be expecting that. It helps reduce confusion, friction, and the learning curve.
