Team Project - 2021

Tiny Bold Strokes

Freelance

Overview

Every artist requires e-commerce to showcase their artworks and Tiny Bold Strokes is one such store. I worked on creating Tiny Bold Storkes' website and branding. The overall theme was to create a website that captured the brand essence while putting the artwork on focus.

My Role

Researcher and Designer

My Team

We are a team of 2 - 1 designer and 1 developer

Timeline

April 2021 - June 2021

The Challenge

How to create a website that highlights the artwork yet holds true to the brand?

Tiny Bold Strokes is all about the artwork. The logo, branding, social media layout are created according to minimalistic principles so that it would not distract the viewer from the artwork. We tried to create a website that reflected the brand's minimalistic principles.

To create an e-commerce site that appeals to art lovers and drives engagement.

The users we were trying to attract are art lovers. There is a huge audience in the engaged community who purchase art. We needed to focus on creating a trustworthy brand

Research

A study on the available online platforms and their depiction of the artist's brand.

During the comparative study of different online platforms available for the artists to sell their artwork, the theme we focused on was how much they enabled the artists to represent their brand. We also focused on customer engagement and how well it catered to the artist's needs.

Etsy: Etsy is one of the most popular online platforms for artists to sell art. It has a good user experience for both the seller and buyer. The platform has a huge customer base and established processed that help drives customer engagement and helped keep track of your shops performance. But due to numerous people selling handmade products on the platform, high chances are that your shop can get lost in that. The platform offered limited customization for shops. This does not allow for brand representation.


Saatchi Art: One of the platforms available exclusively for artists to sell their art to collectors. It has a dedicated customer base of art collectors which provides customer engagement. Since it's an art-focused page, visibility is higher but, it did not have any proper functionality to help the artists keep track of the customer engagement on their shop page. But the platform offers no functionality for customization; this strips out the brand identity from the artist.


Shopify: The platform offers you the option to create your own store. It helps to display the brand but, it was too expensive for starters.

Design Goal and Ideation

A custom platform that can be easily customized, having a good user experience and reflecting the brand.

The key element in the design goal was to craft an online website dedicated to just the brand Tiny Bold Strokes. We wanted to provide a straightforward navigation and check-out process. The design adheres to all the e-commerce process flows that are familiar to the users. Surprises in an e-commerce check-out process might not be the best choice.

Let the art do all the storytelling.

We wanted to minimalize the distraction so that artworks were the center attraction of the pages. Thus we decided on going with a minimalistic approach to the design of the webpage.

Proper functionality for the artists to keep track of the customer engagement metrics for their store.

The platform should have a dedicated space for the artists to track their page's performance and customer engagement.

Final Design

Art shop pages

The website has a minimalistic design landing page as well as store pages. The minimalist design removed all the clutter and brought the focus to the beautiful artworks.

Keeping track of customer engagement.

Another focus was to create a simple dashboard for the artist to track the website's performances and customer engagement patterns.

My Learnings

Some of the things that I learnt while working on this project.

  1. Visual design and UI design principles: This project provided me with the opportunity to learn about visual design, color palette, item placement, and typography.
  2. User expectations: When designing an experience like check out, it's always best to stick to the flow that the users are familiar with since they will be expecting that. It helps reduce confusion, friction, and the learning curve.
