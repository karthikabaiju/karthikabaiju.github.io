Prototype

ColorMo

University of California, Berkeley

ColorMo is a prototype for a color mixing platform that helps defective color vision artists identify and mix colors for their paintings. It provides them with assistance on how to mix the color, it provides feedback to the artist on request and helps identify what color(s) need to be added more to get the identified color.

Background and Inspirations

Let's talk about color blindness

Color blindness or Defective color vision is an inaccessibility that often gets ignored and not talked about a lot. Accessibility checks for color blindness and tools to help the color blind are often talked about in a digital setting. When it comes to more physical and intimate settings such as the vision of an artist, color blindness can mean never being able to work with colors at all.

Peter Milton and Charles Meryon are 2 artists that are an inspiration for this project. When Peter Milton showcased his work, people commented on the warmth of the pinkish landscape, but pink was not the color that Peter thought he was painting with. Soon after he was diagnosed with deuteranopia. Deuteranopia is color blindness resulting from insensitivity to green light, causing confusion about greens, reds, and yellows. Peter sees green as a yellowishly grey color and red as closer to the color of mud. He loved art, but he felt he could not work with colors. Thus his collection of black and white paintings came into being.

Peter Milton: PIAZZA DI SAN MARCO, 2016

Peter Milton: Points of Departure I: Mary's Turn, 1994

Charles Meryon is a 19th-century artist who had a congenital defect in color vision. He found it hard to translate his sketches into paintings. He has only one pastel painting in color and that is full of yellows and blues, the colors preferred by defective color vision artists. He gradually gave up painting in favor of etching.

Charles Meryon: New Caledonia: Large native hut on the road from Balade to Puebo, 1845. www.metmuseum.org

The Design

ColorMo allows you to pick a color from a physical object and helps you mix the color by providing you with the amount of the primary colors required to create the color. It also acts as a color mixing assistant since it provides continuous feedback on the amount of each primary color required to reach the desired color.
